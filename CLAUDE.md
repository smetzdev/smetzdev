# smetz.dev — Claude Guidelines

## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience: potential clients, companies hiring senior developers, and fellow developers. They want to quickly assess whether Sascha is credible, capable, and someone they'd want to work with.

### Brand Personality
Confident and experienced, but genuinely fun — not try-hard. Three words: **Bold. Genuine. Playful.**

Emotional goals: visitors should feel confident in Sascha's skills, sense his personality, and leave with a smile.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Reference**: Brawn GP F1 2009 — shocking neon yellow-on-black, clean and punchy, completely distinctive without being loud.

**Theme**: Pixel-art retro. Custom PixelSerif font, dot-grid patterns, chunky 4px black offset shadows, zero border-radius, all-caps headings. Dark only — no light mode.

**Color palette**:
- `brawn-black` `#142127` — background
- `brawn-gray` `#20303f` — card surfaces
- `brawn-yellow` `#b8fd6e` — primary neon accent
- `brawn-red` `#bd2e40` — emphasis, hover
- `brawn-white` `#feffff` — primary text

**Anti-references** — never:
- Gradient text, glassmorphism, scroll-triggered hero animations, "hero metrics"
- Purple/cyan neon tech-bro dark mode
- Bland corporate blue-white portfolio templates

### Design Principles
1. **Personality over polish** — pixel roughness is part of the character
2. **Neon but clean** — bold color, restrained layouts
3. **No try-hard moments** — purposeful interactions only
4. **Confident whitespace** — big section spacing signals confidence
5. **WCAG AA** — 4.5:1 contrast minimum, keyboard navigable, focus always visible
