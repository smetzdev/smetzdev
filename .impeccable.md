# Impeccable Design Config

## Design Context

### Users
Mixed audience: potential clients hiring a freelancer, companies looking to bring on a senior developer, and fellow developers checking out Sascha's work. Visitors arrive with varying levels of technical literacy but all share the same job-to-be-done: quickly assess whether Sascha is credible, capable, and someone they'd want to work with.

### Brand Personality
Confident and experienced, but genuinely fun — not try-hard. Sascha knows his craft and isn't shy about it, but he's also a person you'd actually want to grab a coffee with. The vibe is "seasoned professional who doesn't take himself too seriously."

Three words: **Bold. Genuine. Playful.**

Emotional goals: visitors should feel confident in Sascha's skills, get a sense of his personality, and leave with a smile.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Reference**: Brawn GP F1 team — 2009 World Championship season. Shocking neon yellow-on-black livery that looked completely out of place and utterly distinctive. Clean, punchy, unexpected. Not flashy for its own sake — it just *worked*.

**Theme**: Pixel-art retro. Custom PixelSerif font, dot-grid patterns, chunky 4px black offset shadows, zero border-radius, all-caps headings. The rawness is intentional.

**Color palette** (Brawn GP-inspired):
- `brawn-black` `#142127` — background base
- `brawn-gray` `#20303f` — card/surface layer
- `brawn-yellow` `#b8fd6e` — primary accent (the neon)
- `brawn-red` `#bd2e40` — emphasis, hover states
- `brawn-white` `#feffff` — primary text

**Theme**: Dark only. No light mode.

**Anti-references** — this should never look like:
- Over-designed AI slop: no gradient text, no glassmorphism, no scroll-triggered hero animations, no "hero metrics" section
- Dark mode cliché: no purple/cyan neon tech-bro aesthetic
- Safe corporate portfolio: no bland blue-white agency templates, no stock photo vibes

### Design Principles
1. **Personality over polish** — A rough pixel edge beats a smooth generic surface. Imperfection is part of the character.
2. **Neon but clean** — High contrast, bold color use, but layouts stay legible and uncluttered. The yellow pops because everything around it is restrained.
3. **No try-hard moments** — Avoid clever-for-its-own-sake animations or effects. Every interaction should feel natural and purposeful.
4. **Confident whitespace** — Big spacing (`space-y-24` between sections) signals confidence. Don't cram.
5. **WCAG AA accessibility** — 4.5:1 contrast minimum for all text, keyboard navigable, focus indicators always visible. Brawn-yellow on brawn-black is the accessible accent.
